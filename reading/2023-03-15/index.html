Interesting choices in Zig

https://ziglang.org/documentation/0.10.1/#Error-Set-Type

I think that Zig's management of errors is interesting. They essentially scope errors to the package level, and require each dependency to manage propagation of other underlying errors, such as I/O.

Since you can't scope down, though, it means that you can't expose some underlying generic error type like "couldn't write file". I guess this is similar to how Rust does it, though - exposing the error type of an external crate is generally bad practice.

The other interesting design decision here is that the error type is just a mask over a u16, instead of something more powerful like a proper datatype. Then again, Golang just uses strings, and C just uses ints.
