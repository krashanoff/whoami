Entropy

Frameworks are the heart of the Internet these days. React, Vue, Angular, Next, Svelte, Solid, ... it doesn't really matter. There's a framework getting shoved into your frontend, and it will add some bootstrapped bloat to the site.

Similarly, static linking seems to be the most popular way for building software in modern languages like Rust, Go, Haskell, OCaml, ... Bloat haunts software design. Java sees a hellish amount of design bloat.

But there is also a kind of bloat that pervades language design. Haskell is marred by its {-# LANGUAGE ... #-} pragmas, and C++ seems to be getting the grave robber treatment with its additions of parametric constraints pulled from Go's implicit interfaces. Rust, too, is marked by strange decisions like its implementation of async/await.

The second law of thermodynamics: the entropy of any closed system cannot decrease.
